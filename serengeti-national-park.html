Serengeti National Park

The Endless Plains of Africa

Witness the Great Migration

The Serengeti National Park is Tanzania's oldest park and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is famous for its annual migration of over 1.5 million white-bearded (or brindled) wildebeest and 250,000 zebra and for its numerous Nile crocodile and honey badger.

Game Drive

Explore the vast Serengeti plains on guided game drives. Spot the Big Five (lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, rhino) and witness predator-prey interactions in one of the most wildlife-rich areas on Earth.

The Great Migration

Witness the world's largest overland migration. Millions of wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles traverse the Serengeti ecosystem in search of fresh grazing, braving crocodile-infested rivers and stalking predators.

Hot Air Balloon Safari

Float silently above the plains at sunrise. A hot air balloon safari offers a unique perspective of the vast Serengeti landscape and its wildlife, followed by a champagne breakfast in the bush.
