Tarangire National Park

Land of Giants

Elephants and Baobabs

Tarangire National Park is famous for its high density of elephants and giant baobab trees. During the dry season, the Tarangire River becomes a magnet for thirsty wildlife, creating spectacular viewing opportunities.

Elephant Herds

Witness herds of up to 300 elephants digging for water in the dry riverbed or resting under the shade of ancient baobab trees. It's an elephant lover's paradise.

Bird Watching

With over 550 bird species, Tarangire is a birdwatcher's haven. Spot colorful lovebirds, starlings, and raptors in the varied habitats of swamps and savannahs.

Walking Safaris

Experience the bush on foot. Accompanied by an armed ranger, learn about the smaller details of the ecosystem, from tracks and signs to medicinal plants.
