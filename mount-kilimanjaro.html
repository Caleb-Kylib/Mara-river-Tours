Mount Kilimanjaro

The Roof of Africa

Conquer The Summit

Rising majestically from the African plains, Mount Kilimanjaro is the world's highest free-standing mountain at 5,895 meters. Its snow-capped peak is a beacon for adventurers worldwide, offering a challenging yet achievable trek through five distinct climate zones.

Summit Trekking

Embark on a multi-day journey to Uhuru Peak. Choose from various routes like Machame (Wiskey), Marangu (Coca-Cola), or Lemosho, each offering different scenery and acclimatization profiles.

Unique Landscapes

Traverse through lush rainforests, heath and moorland, alpine deserts, and finally, the arctic summit zone. Marvel at giant senecios, lobelias, and the spectacular glaciers near the crater rim.

Acclimatization Walks

"Pole Pole" (slowly, slowly) is the motto. Our itineraries are designed with acclimatization days to maximize your chances of reaching the summit safely and enjoying the experience.

Climb Kilimanjaro

Join our expert team for a safe and successful expedition to the top of Africa.

Machame Route (7 Days)

The "Whiskey Route" is popular for its scenic beauty and good acclimatization profile. It approaches from the south and descends via Mweka.

Itinerary
  • Day 1: Machame Gate to Machame Camp
  • Day 2: Machame Camp to Shira 2 Camp
  • Day 3: Shira 2 to Lava Tower to Barranco Camp
  • Day 4: Barranco Wall to Karanga Camp
  • Day 5: Karanga to Barafu Camp (Base Camp)
  • Day 6: Summit Night! Uhuru Peak & descent to Mweka
  • Day 7: Mweka Camp to Mweka Gate
