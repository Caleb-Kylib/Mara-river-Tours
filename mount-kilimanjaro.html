Why Climb With Mara Tours?

7-Day Machame Route Itinerary

Elevation: 1,800m to 2,835m | Time: 5-7 hours

After registering at the gate, we begin our trek through the lush rainforest. Watch for colobus monkeys and exotic birds in the canopy. We camp at Machame Camp for the night.

Elevation: 2,835m to 3,750m | Time: 4-6 hours

We leave the rainforest and enter the heath and moorland zone. The route is steeper, but the views of the Shira Plateau are rewarding. We camp at Shira 2.

Elevation: 3,750m to 4,600m to 3,900m | Time: 6-8 hours

This is a crucial acclimatization day. We climb up to Lava Tower (4,600m) for lunch and then descend to Barranco Camp. This "climb high, sleep low" strategy improves success rates.

Elevation: 3,900m to 3,995m | Time: 4-5 hours

We start by climbing the Great Barranco Wall – a steep but non-technical scramble. The rest of the day is a short hike through the Karanga Valley, allowing for rest and acclimatization.

Elevation: 3,995m to 4,673m | Time: 3-4 hours

A short but steep hike to Barafu Camp (Base Camp). We arrive early for an early dinner and sleep, preparing for the midnight summit push.

Elevation: 4,673m to 5,895m (Summit) down to 3,100m | Time: 11-14 hours

We start at midnight. The climb to Stella Point (5,756m) is the most mentally and physically challenging. At sunrise, we reach Uhuru Peak – the Roof of Africa! After celebrating, we descend all the way to Mweka Camp.

Elevation: 3,100m to 1,640m | Time: 3-4 hours

A gentle trek through the rainforest to Mweka Gate, where successful climbers receive their summit certificates. We drive back to the hotel for a well-deserved shower and celebration dinner.
