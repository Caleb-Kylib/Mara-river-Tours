Ngorongoro Crater

Africa's Garden of Eden

A Natural Wonder

The Ngorongoro Crater is the world's largest inactive, intact and unfilled volcanic caldera. Home to over 25,000 large animals, it offers the best chance to see the Big Five in a single day, all set against the dramatic backdrop of the crater walls.

Crater Floor Game Drive

Descend 600 meters into the crater floor for a spectacular game drive. The enclosed ecosystem creates a unique environment where wildlife is abundant and accustomed to vehicles, allowing for incredible photographic opportunities.

Olduvai Gorge Visit

Visit the "Cradle of Mankind," one of the most important paleoanthropological sites in the world. Learn about early human evolution and the discoveries made by the Leakey family.

Maasai Cultural Visit

The Ngorongoro Conservation Area is a multiple land use area where Maasai pastoralists co-exist with wild animals. Visit a traditional boma to learn about their culture, traditions, and way of life.

A comprehensive safari exploring the crater floor, the rim, and the surrounding highlands.
